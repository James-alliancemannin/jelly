The Framework 03
03

Why perception
beats features.

Rory Sutherland observed that the subjective value of something is not determined by its functional attributes, but by its perceived attributes. We design for both — and we know which one actually converts.

01

Trust precedes logic

Visitors form a judgement within 50 milliseconds of arrival. Before reading a word. Before evaluating an offer. The visual language either earns trust or destroys it — and no amount of copy can recover what a bad first impression lost.

02

Authority is engineered

The signals that communicate expertise — typography hierarchy, negative space, structural precision, editorial restraint — are not accidents. They are decisions. We make those decisions with intent, not instinct.

03

Friction destroys conversion

Every unnecessary click, every confusing navigation, every second of load time is a decision to abandon. Friction is not neutral. It is an active vote against your brand — paid for by the visitor, charged to you.

04

The AI era rewards structure

Large language models are now the first point of discovery for millions of decisions. Sites that are semantically precise, factually authoritative, and structured for machine comprehension win citations, recommendations, and referrals at a scale Google alone never provided.
